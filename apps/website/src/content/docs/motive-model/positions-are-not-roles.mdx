Find yourself in this page first. The model is easiest to grasp from the job title you already hold.

A **position** is a job title — PM, Designer, Engineer, QA. A **role** is something narrower: the kind of contribution you make *right now*, defined by what you want from the work. Pre-AI, a position was one fixed **bundle** — one motive, one object, one angle — because production was scarce and focus was finite:

- PM **framed** the product.
- Engineer **built** the code.
- Designer **built** the UX.
- QA **evaluated** the quality.

One title, one job. That held because nobody had spare capacity to do a second kind of work well.

## What AI changes

With delegates supplying production, the bundle comes apart. Each position can now act as **more than one motive**, and reach past its default object. The title becomes a *default*, not a boundary.

| Position | Default bundle (object · motive) | Motives AI now opens |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **PM** | product · intend | Build a prototype with a delegate; encode product knowledge for reuse |
| **Designer** | product · build the UX | Own the *why*; own the design system; own the design corpus |
| **Engineer** | product · build the code | Structure the codebase; own conventions; reach into process and toolchain |
| **QA** | product · evaluate quality | Own acceptance criteria; curate golden sets; probe adversarially |

The motives named here — intend, build, structure, accumulate, evaluate — are the **[four actors and their two faces](/motive-model/four-actors/)**. This table is just the door into them.

## QA is the sharpest case

QA's default is **evaluation** — the trained quality specialist whose whole job is judging the product. When delegates write the checks, QA stops being the one who *writes* tests and becomes the one who **owns the acceptance contract and curates the corpus it draws from**. The motive didn't change — it was always about the product working. What changed is that production got cheap, so the scarce, valuable part — deciding what "good" means and guarding it — is now the whole job.

## Two things the old org chart no longer says

<Aside type="note" title="There is no Gatekeeper position">
Judging isn't a job someone owns — it's what *any* expertise does when you turn it backward on a result. Every position reviews whatever it touches. Review is everyone's part-time act, not one person's title. (More in [Two faces and the gate](/motive-model/faces-and-the-gate/).)
</Aside>

<Aside type="tip" title="The title is a starting point, not a fence">
An Engineer who notices three features each rolling their own auth and extracts one shared path has stepped into a *structuring* motive — same person, different role, same minute. The model lets you name that without a promotion or a re-org.
</Aside>
