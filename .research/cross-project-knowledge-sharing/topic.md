# Cross-Project Knowledge Sharing (stub — June 2026)

> **Status: not yet researched.** Spun out of the Actor–Delegate Model work (`.research/actor-delegate-model/`), where the Curator was defined as the actor whose product is *knowledge that outlives any single product*. This topic investigates how that knowledge actually crosses project boundaries.

## Question

When knowledge is meant to outlive the product it came from, how does it get shared across projects? What forms does transferable knowledge take, and how is each packaged, versioned, discovered, and adopted?

## Scope

**In scope:** the concrete manifestations of a Curator's corpus as cross-project assets — templates/scaffolds, conventions and lint rules, libraries, agent skills, agent plugins, starter kits, golden paths; their packaging, versioning, discovery, and adoption mechanics; the failure modes (drift, staleness, low adoption).

**Out of scope:** the Actor–Delegate Model framework itself (parent topic).

## Source angles (to investigate)

- Software reuse and DRY *across* codebases (not within one)
- Inner-source / internal open source
- Platform engineering golden paths and self-service templates (Team Topologies)
- Package/registry ecosystems and versioning (semver, deprecation, discovery)
- Agent skills / plugins as packaged, transferable knowledge artifacts
- Scaffolding/templating tools and their adoption patterns
